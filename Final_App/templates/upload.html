This website is part of a Final Project from CPSC 464/564: Algorithms and their Societal Implications titled BALANCE: Boundary Adjustment for Local Academic Needs and Community Equity

This project develops a scalable, fair and multi-factor approach to school zone redistricting to promote socioeconomic integration and reflect the overall demographics of a school district. We design a method that balances community stability, geographic convenience, and demographic parity. Finally, we build a robust algorithm using Mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) that can optimize multiple factors, generate fair boundaries based on various metrics, and offer flexible data-driven redistricting solutions that school systems can adapt to their unique needs.

Matthew Shu, Sasha Spiegel, Savik Kinger, Aadi Krishna, Noah Dee, R. Tyler Schroder

Professor: Nisheeth K. Vishnoi
